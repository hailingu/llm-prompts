## 1. The Strategic Role of the War
In the context of "Operation Epic Fury" (US/Israel vs. Iran), the Russia-Ukraine war shifts from the *primary* global theater to the **"Strategic Pivot"**. It acts as a **zero-sum resource sink** for the West.

### The "Second Front" Dividend
For Moscow, the conflict in the Middle East is a strategic windfall (a "Black Swan" in their favor):
- **Resource Diversion**: Every Patriot battery, 155mm shell, and satellite hour dedicated to protecting Israel/Gulf is one less available for Kyiv.
- **Attention Deficit**: Western political capital is finite. US elections (Nov 2026) will focus on "Gas Prices" and "Iran War", pushing Ukraine to the backburner.
- **Sanctions Erosion**: High oil prices (>$150/bbl) obliterate the G7 Price Cap mechanism. Russia can sell at huge discounts to India/China and still make record profits.

## 2. Russia's Strategic Objectives (March 2026)

### A. Military: The "Opportunistic Offensive"
*   **Goal**: Break the stalemate in Donbas while the West is distracted.
*   **Tactic**: Launch a high-intensity "Spring Offensive" (March-April 2026).
*   **Logic**: Anticipating an "Ammo Starvation" period in Ukraine (3-6 months) as US supply lines prioritize CENTCOM.
*   **Weaponry**: Utilizing North Korean stocks (which are now prioritized for Russia as Iran needs its own production for survival, creating a potential friction point, but currently Iran fights an air war while Russia fights a ground war).

### B. Economic: The "War Premium" Harvest
*   **Goal**: Maximize revenue to insulate the economy for the long haul.
*   **Action**: 
    *   **Energy**: Refuse to increase output within OPEC+, citing "technical constraints", keeping global prices high to punish European economies.
    *   **Trade**: Position Russia as the *only* stable energy supplier to China (Power of Siberia 2 pipeline acceleration).

### C. Diplomatic: The "Quagmire Facilitator"
*   **Goal**: Ensure the US stays bogged down in the Middle East for years.
*   **Action**:
    *   **Intelligence Sharing**: Provide IRGC remnants with satellite data on US fleet movements (deniable capability).
    *   **Air Defense**: Potentially "leak" S-400 components or electronic warfare tech to Iran via proxies to increase cost for US air power.
    *   **Narrative**: Frame the Iran strike as "Western Colonial Aggression", rallying the Global South against the "US-dominated order".

## 3. Risks for Russia
*   **Iran Collapse**: If the Islamic Republic collapses too quickly, the US wins a major victory and can pivot *back* to Europe with renewed confidence and resources. Russia *needs* a stalemate in Iran, not a quick US victory.
*   **China's Pivot**: If China feels the energy shock is too severe, Beijing might pressure Moscow to help stabilize the Middle East, creating friction in the "No Limits" partnership.
