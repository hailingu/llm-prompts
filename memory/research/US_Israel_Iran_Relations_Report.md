# Report: The History and Current Status of US-Israel-Iran Relations
**Date:** March 3, 2026

## Executive Summary
The relationship between the United States, Israel, and Iran has evolved from a strategic trilateral alliance during the Cold War to one of the most volatile geopolitical fault lines in the 21st century. Following the 1979 Islamic Revolution, the US-Israel-Iran triangle shifted to a binary conflict: the US and Israel consolidated a strategic partnership, while Iran emerged as their primary regional adversary. 

As of March 2026, the situation has escalated into direct military confrontation, marked by the joint US-Israeli "Operation Epic Fury" launched in late February 2026, which reportedly resulted in the death of Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.

---

## 1. Before the Pahlavi Dynasty (19th Century - Early 20th Century)
*Benevolent Distance and Missionary contact*

*   **US-Iran:** Relations were largely non-political and defined by educational and medical missionaries. The US was viewed favorably by Iranians as a "third force" free from the colonial baggage of Russia and Great Britain.
    *   **1856:** First distinct diplomatic contact; the Treaty of Friendship and Commerce was signed.
    *   **1911:** The outcome of the Morgan Shuster mission (an American treasurer-general appointed to organize Persian finances) created deep goodwill. Shuster was forced out by Russian pressure, cementing an image of the US as a supporter of Iranian sovereignty.
*   **Israel-Iran:** Non-existent as state relations (Israel was not yet established). However, the Persian Jewish community (one of the oldest in the world) lived in relative stability, though subject to occasional discrimination. Jewish immigration to Palestine increased with the rise of Zionism.

## 2. During the Pahlavi Dynasty (1925-1979)
*The Era of Strategic Cooperation*

This period is characterized by the "Twin Pillars" policy, where the US relied on Iran and Saudi Arabia to secure the Persian Gulf.

### US-Iran Relations
*   **WWII & Post-War:** The Allied invasion of Iran (1941) to secure supply lines to the USSR brought the US heavily into Iranian affairs.
*   **1953 Coup (Operation Ajax):** A defining turning point. The CIA and MI6 orchestrated the overthrow of democratically elected Prime Minister Mohammad Mosaddegh to reinstate the Shah, securing Western oil interests but sowing deep seeds of anti-American sentiment.
*   **Cold War Alliance:** The Shah became the US conflict's primary guardian in the Gulf. Massive arms sales (F-14 Tomcats) and nuclear technology transfers (Atoms for Peace) defined the era.

### Israel-Iran Relations
*   **De Facto Recognition:** Iran was the second Muslim-majority country to recognize Israel (after Turkey) in 1950.
*   **The Periphery Doctrine:** Israel cultivated alliances with non-Arab states (Iran, Turkey, Ethiopia) to counter Arab hostility.
*   **Cooperation:**
    *   **Energy:** Iran supplied oil to Israel when Arab states imposed embargoes (e.g., after the Six-Day War). The Eilat-Ashkelon pipeline was a joint venture.
    *   **Security:** SAVAK (Iran's intelligence service) and Mossad cooperated closely.
    *   **Project Flower:** A secret joint military project to develop advanced missile systems.

## 3. After the Pahlavi Dynasty (1979 Revolution)
*The Great Rupture*

The Islamic Revolution transformed Iran from a status quo power into a revolutionary state seeking to export its ideology, fundamentally altering the region.

*   **1979 Revolution:** Ayatollah Khomeini labeled the US the "Great Satan" and Israel the "Little Satan". Israel's embassy was handed over to the PLO.
*   **Hostage Crisis (1979-1981):** 52 US diplomats were held for 444 days, severing diplomatic ties permanently.
*   **The Proxy War Era (1982-2023):**
    *   **Lebanon:** Iran founded and funded Hezbollah, which fought a long war against Israeli occupation in South Lebanon.
    *   **The Nuclear Shadow:** From the 2000s onward, Israel viewed Iran's nuclear program as an existential threat. The US led sanctions regimes (UN, EU, US) to cripple Iran's economy.
    *   **The JCPOA (2015):** A brief diplomatic thaw under Obama, dismantled by the Trump administration in 2018 ("Maximum Pressure" campaign).
    *   **The "Shadow War":** Cyberattacks (Stuxnet), assassinations of Iranian scientists, and Israeli strikes on Iranian entrenchments in Syria.

## 4. Current Situation (2024 - March 2026)
*Escalation to Direct War*

The period from 2024 to early 2026 saw the collapse of containment strategies and the outbreak of direct hostilities.

### Key Developments
*   **2024-2025 Regional Spillover:** The aftermath of the Gaza war led to entrenched multi-front conflicts involving Iran's "Axis of Resistance" (Hezbollah, Houthis, militias in Iraq/Syria).
*   **Political Shift:** The return of the Trump administration in the US (inaugurated Jan 2025) signaled a return to "Maximum Pressure" combined with willingness for high-stakes deal-making.
*   **Failed Negotiations (April 2025):** 
    *   President Trump sent a letter to Supreme Leader Khamenei setting a 60-day deadline for a new comprehensive "nuclear peace agreement".
    *   Talks began in Geneva on April 12, 2025, but collapsed by June.
*   **The June War (June 2025):** Following the deadline's expiration, Israel and the US launched limited strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities (June 22, 2025), aiming to degrade enrichment capabilities. Iran retaliated with missile barrages.

### The 2026 Escalation ("Operation Epic Fury")
As of March 3, 2026, the region is in a state of major conflict.

*   **February 28, 2026:** The US and Israel launched **"Operation Epic Fury"**, a massive, coordinated air campaign targeting regime command and control, nuclear sites, and IRGC infrastructure.
*   **Leadership Decapitation:** Credible reports indicate that **Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was killed** in a strike on Tehran on Feb 28. The Defense Minister and IRGC Commander were also reported killed.
*   **Current Diplomatic Stance (March 2026):**
    *   **USA:** President Trump has declared the operation a "massive and ongoing" campaign for "regime change" and "total denuclearization".
    *   **Israel:** Forces are on maximum alert. The Israeli government describes the operation as a "preventive war" to eliminate the existential threat finally.
    *   **Iran:** The country is in chaos and shock. Surviving leadership elements have vowed "apocalyptic retaliation" and have launched ballistic missiles at US bases in the region.
