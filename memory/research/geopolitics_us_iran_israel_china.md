## 3. 关系分析 (Relationship Analysis)

### A. 美国 - 伊朗 (US-Iran)
- **Current State**: **Total War / Regime Change**.
- **Dynamics**: The US has abandoned containment for direct military intervention. The goal is the complete dismantlement of the Islamic Republic's current leadership structure and nuclear program.
- **Key Driver**: Failure of 2025 Geneva talks and the subsequent "June War" escalation.
- **Strategic Mechanism**: "Regime Legitimacy Mechanism" - Domestic pressure in both US (new admin) and Iran pushed towards hardline policies.

### B. 以色列 - 伊朗 (Israel-Iran)
- **Current State**: **Existential Conflict**.
- **Dynamics**: Israel is executing a "preventive war" strategy ("Operation Epic Fury"). The focus is on decapitation strikes (Khamenei confirmed killed) and destroying nuclear infrastructure.
- **Key Driver**: "Security Dilemma Mechanism" - Iranian "forward defense" via proxies was viewed by Israel as an imminent existential threat, triggering preemptive strikes.

### C. 中国 - 伊朗 (China-Iran)
- **Current State**: **Strategic Paralysis**.
- **Dynamics**: China's 25-year strategic partnership is effectively paralyzed. The decapitation of the Iranian regime threatens China's energy investments and BRI projects.
- **Economic Vulnerability**: The "Economic Vulnerability Mechanism" (from Timeline) is active - war disrupts oil flow, spiking insurance premiums and threatening China's energy security.
- **Dilemma**: Beijing faces a choice between supporting a collapsing regime or cutting losses. Likely stance is diplomatic condemnation of "hegemony" without direct military intervention.

### D. 中国 - 以色列 (China-Israel)
- **Current State**: **Frozen / Hostile**.
- **Dynamics**: Israel's alignment with US "regime change" policies directly undermines China's regional stability goals. Tech cooperation (semiconductors, AI) is likely terminated under US pressure and wartime conditions.
- **Friction**: China's likely support for an immediate ceasefire helps Iran regroup, which Israel views as hostile.

### E. 美国 - 中国 (Middle East Context)
- **Current State**: **Indirect Conflict**.
- **Dynamics**: The US war disrupts China's energy security. The US effectively challenges China's "peacemaker" narrative (Saudi-Iran deal) by demonstrating that hard power still dictates regional outcomes.
- **Risk**: Potential for US naval blockades in the Strait of Hormuz to serve as leverage against Chinese economy.
